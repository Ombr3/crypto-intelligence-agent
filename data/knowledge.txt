Ethereum (ETH) is the second largest cryptocurrency by market cap.
It introduced smart contracts, enabling decentralized applications (dApps).
Ethereum moved from Proof of Work to Proof of Stake in September 2022 (The Merge).
This reduced Ethereum's energy consumption by approximately 99.95%.
Ethereum's native token ETH is used to pay for gas fees on the network.

Bitcoin (BTC) is the first and largest cryptocurrency by market cap.
It was created by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009.
Bitcoin has a fixed supply cap of 21 million coins.
Bitcoin halving events occur every 4 years, reducing miner rewards by half.
The most recent halving was in April 2024, reducing block reward to 3.125 BTC.

Funding rate is a periodic payment between long and short traders in perpetual futures.
Positive funding rate means longs pay shorts — market is bullish/overleveraged long.
Negative funding rate means shorts pay longs — market is bearish/overleveraged short.
Extreme funding rates (above 0.1% or below -0.1%) often precede price reversals.

Open interest is the total value of outstanding futures contracts.
Rising open interest with rising price confirms bullish trend.
Rising open interest with falling price confirms bearish trend.
Falling open interest suggests traders are closing positions.

Fear and Greed Index measures market sentiment from 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed).
Values above 75 suggest the market may be overheated.
Values below 25 suggest potential buying opportunities.
